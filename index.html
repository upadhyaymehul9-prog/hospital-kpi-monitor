① What is Hospital KPI Monitor?

Hospital KPI Monitor is a free, browser-based tool for hospital administrators, quality managers, clinical leads, and finance teams. It lets any healthcare facility instantly calculate, benchmark, and report on critical performance indicators — with no software installation, login, or data upload required.

② How to Use the App

The app has two modes accessible from the top navigation bar:

⚡ Quick Calculator
Pick any KPI card, enter 2–3 numbers, get an instant result with colour-coded benchmark. Best for spot-checking a single metric.
1Click "⚡ Quick Calc" in the nav
2Filter by category or browse all 20 KPIs
3Click any KPI card to open the calculator
4Enter numbers — result appears instantly
5🟢 Good · 🟡 Review · 🔴 Critical
📊 Full Dashboard
Enter all hospital data once. All KPIs auto-calculate. Export a clean PDF scorecard for meetings and board reports.
1Click "📊 Full Dashboard" in the nav
2Enter hospital name and report period
3Fill in available data fields (skip unknowns)
4Click "⚡ Auto-Calculate All KPIs"
5Review results and click "🖨 Print / PDF"
💡 PDF Export tip: In the print dialog, set destination to "Save as PDF" (Chrome/Edge). The scorecard auto-hides buttons and shows only your KPI results.
③ Efficiency KPIs

Efficiency KPIs measure how well the hospital utilises its physical and operational resources.

Bed Occupancy Rate (BOR)Target 80–85%
Occupied Beds ÷ Total Available Beds × 100
Measures how full the hospital is. Too low wastes resources; above 90% risks patient safety and staff burnout.
Avg Length of Stay (ALOS)Target < 4–6 days
Total Inpatient Days ÷ Total Discharges
Shorter stays indicate efficient clinical pathways. Long ALOS raises costs, infection risk, and reduces bed availability.
Bed Turnover Rate (BTR)Target 30–50 pts/bed
Total Discharges ÷ Total Available Beds
How many patients each bed serves per period. Low BTR may signal inefficiency or excessive length of stay.
ICU Occupancy Rate NEWTarget 60–85%
Occupied ICU Beds ÷ Total ICU Beds × 100
Above 85% signals ICU capacity crisis. Monitor daily and have escalation protocols in place.
OPD Avg Wait TimeTarget < 30 min
Total OPD Wait Time ÷ Total OPD Patients
Long waits reduce patient satisfaction. Over 60 minutes indicates serious triage or scheduling problems.
OPD On-Time RateTarget > 85%
Patients Seen on Time ÷ Total OPD Visits × 100
Percentage of OPD patients seen within the target time. Low rates reflect scheduling or capacity problems.
Surgery Cancellation RateTarget < 5%
Cancelled Surgeries ÷ Scheduled Surgeries × 100
High cancellations waste theatre time and delay patient care. Review pre-operative assessment processes.
④ Quality & Safety KPIs

Quality KPIs assess clinical outcomes and patient safety — closely monitored by accreditation bodies worldwide.

Gross Mortality RateTarget < 1%
Total Deaths ÷ Total Admissions × 100
In-hospital death rate. Review alongside case mix — ICU and oncology wards have naturally higher rates.
30-Day Readmission RateTarget < 5%
Readmissions ÷ Discharges × 100
High readmissions suggest inadequate discharge planning. Review follow-up care and patient education.
HAI RateTarget < 1%
HAI Cases ÷ Admissions × 100
Healthcare-associated infections are largely preventable. High rates indicate infection control gaps.
Patient Fall RateTarget < 1 per 100 pts
Falls ÷ Admissions × 100
Falls cause harm and extend stays. Review fall risk assessments and patient mobility protocols.
Surgical Complication RateTarget < 2%
Complications ÷ Completed Surgeries × 100
Complications increase length of stay and costs. Track trends per surgeon and procedure type.
⑤ Staffing KPIs

Staffing KPIs ensure adequate human resources for safe, quality care. Understaffing is directly linked to adverse patient events.

Patient:Nurse RatioIdeal 1:3–4, Max 1:6
Occupied Beds ÷ Nurses on Shift
Above 1:6 significantly increases adverse event risk. ICU should maintain 1:1–2.
Doctor:Patient RatioTarget < 10 pts/doc
Occupied Beds ÷ Doctors on Duty
Very high ratios reduce quality of clinical assessment and decision-making time.
Staff Absence RateTarget < 3%
Absent Days ÷ Scheduled Days × 100
High absence signals burnout or morale issues. Review shift patterns and workload distribution.
Staff Productivity NEWTarget 15–40 pts/staff
Total Patients ÷ Total Staff
Low productivity may indicate overstaffing. Very high signals burnout risk — review staffing levels and case mix.
Lab & Diagnostics
Lab Turnaround RateTarget > 90%
Results <1hr ÷ Total Tests × 100
Measures how quickly lab results are available. Delays impact clinical decisions and bed flow.
Critical Lab Notify RateTarget 100%
Critical Results Notified ÷ Total Critical Results × 100
100% notification is a patient safety standard. Any missed critical value is a reportable risk event.
⑥ Financial KPIs

Financial KPIs ensure operational sustainability. Review monthly and benchmark against the same period last year.

Cost Per Patient DayCompare vs budget
Total Operating Cost ÷ Total Inpatient Days
Rising Cost/Day without revenue increase signals overspend. Compare vs previous periods.
Claim Rejection RateTarget < 5%
Rejected Claims ÷ Total Claims × 100
Each rejection is deferred or lost revenue. Indicates billing errors or documentation gaps.
Drug Cost %Target < 20%
Drug Cost ÷ Total Operating Cost × 100
High drug cost % may indicate overuse or non-generic prescribing patterns.
Avg Revenue per Patient (ARPP) NEWVaries by hospital type
Total Revenue ÷ Total Patients
Low ARPP alongside high volume may indicate over-reliance on low-value services. Review case mix and pricing.
⑦ Revenue Performance KPIs NEW

These KPIs reveal revenue yield, operational profitability, payer mix sustainability, and departmental performance.

Avg Revenue per Occupied Bed (AROB)Higher = better
Total Revenue ÷ Total Occupied Bed Days
Revenue yield per bed-day — not just whether beds are full, but how much each occupied bed earns. Essential for pricing strategy.
EBITDA MarginTarget 10–15%
EBITDA ÷ Total Revenue × 100
The clearest single measure of whether the hospital covers its running costs before financing and depreciation.
Dept Contribution MarginTarget > 25%
(Dept Revenue − Direct Cost) ÷ Dept Revenue × 100
Identifies which specialties drive profitability. Loss-making departments need immediate pricing or cost review.
Revenue Mix RatioBalanced mix = lower risk
Each payer type ÷ Total Revenue × 100
Breaks revenue into Private, Govt Scheme, and Insurance. High private = pricing control. High scheme = stable but low margin.
💡 Key Insight: Compare AROB vs Cost/Day. If your revenue per occupied bed is lower than your cost per patient day, you are losing money on every inpatient — regardless of occupancy rate.

⑨ Tips & Best Practices
01
Use Full Dashboard for monthly QA meetings
Enter all data before your monthly Quality or Board meeting. Print the PDF scorecard as a one-page handout for leadership.
02
Use Quick Calc for ward-level spot checks
Ward managers can check P:N Ratio, OPD Wait, or BOR during morning rounds without filling the full form.
03
Compare AROB vs Cost/Day for financial health
If AROB is lower than Cost/Day, you lose money on every occupied bed — regardless of how high occupancy is.
04
ICU BOR above 85% = act now
ICU capacity crises develop fast. Monitor ICU BOR daily and have clear escalation protocols.
05
Track trends, not just snapshots
One month has limited value. Keep a monthly log to identify improving or worsening patterns over time.
06
Leave unknown fields blank — no mandatory fields
The app skips KPIs where data is missing. You never get an error. Just fill in what you have.
07
Revenue Mix tells you about pricing power
High private mix = pricing flexibility. High scheme = stable but low margin. Balanced mix reduces concentration risk.
08
Share freely — zero privacy risk
The app stores nothing. Share the URL with any colleague, clinic, or hospital with complete confidence.
